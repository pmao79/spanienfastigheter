# Nueva Andalucía Real Estate Data

## Population Overview

**Nueva Andalucía has approximately 21,530 residents as of 2023**, with seasonal fluctuations during summer months.[1][4] The population has grown significantly over the past decade, increasing from 14,441 in 2010 to 18,652 in 2020.[1] The neighborhood comprises 10,444 males and 11,086 females.[1]

Nueva Andalucía is part of Marbella, which itself has experienced rapid growth, reaching 172,063 inhabitants by September 2025, representing approximately 20 new residents per day in 2025.[2] Marbella is the second most populous municipality in Málaga Province.[3]

## Neighborhood Character

Nueva Andalucía is characterized as a **well-planned, family-friendly area** with wide streets and comprehensive amenities despite being within walking distance of the vibrant Puerto Banús marina.[4] The neighborhood was developed beginning in 1965 by Madrid developer José Banús Masdeu and experienced rapid construction until 1989 and again during the 2000s.[5]

The area is primarily residential but avoids the "major hustle and bustle, traffic and noise" typical of central Marbella.[4]

## Market Data and Housing Development

Marbella experienced substantial employment growth, with Social Security contributors increasing from 70,040 in September 2021 to 81,390 by September 2025—an average of approximately 2,840 additional workers annually.[2] The municipality is actively developing affordable housing (VPOs), with 399 units under construction or approved in San Pedro Alcántara alone, targeting 800 VPOs within five years.[2]
