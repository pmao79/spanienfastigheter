**Sotogrande, a gated residential community in San Roque municipality, Cádiz Province, Andalusia, Spain, has an estimated permanent population of around 10,000 residents across its 2,000-hectare area, with the core locality at 3,098 (2024 estimate) and the broader Guadiaro-Sotogrande urban area at 10,357 (2023 estimate).[1][2][3][10]**

### Population Details
- **Core locality (Sotogrande, San Roque):** 3,098 (2024 est.), up 9.9% annually from 2020; 2,932 males/females (2023).[2]
- **Guadiaro-Sotogrande urban area:** 10,357 (2023 est.), from 8,381 (2010); 5,066 males, 5,291 females.[1]
- **Overall area:** ~10,000 permanent residents, tripling in summer due to tourism; San Roque municipality grew 92% to 33,018 (2022).[3]
- Conflicting estimates exist (e.g., 6,000+ or 3,722), but INE-sourced data (Instituto Nacional de Estadística) prioritizes ~10,000 as most consistent and recent.[1][2][3][5][8]
- Area: 12.3 km² (one source); 2,000 hectares total private estate with ~2,300 villas, 2,500 apartments/townhouses.[5][6][10]

### Why Swedes Love It
Sotogrande attracts Swedes due to its **strong expat community**, mild Mediterranean climate (vs. Nordic winters), English/Scandinavian-speaking services, top schools like Sotogrande International School (Forbes top 20 in Spain), and luxury sports (golf/polo).[3][7] Direct flights from Stockholm/Malmö to Gibraltar/Málaga, plus a safe, gated environment with amenities like health centers and high-end dining, mirror Swedish quality-of-life preferences while offering sun, sea, and investment potential.[3][7]

### Market Data
- **Housing stock:** ~2,300 villas, ~2,500 apartments/townhouses; focuses on high-end buyers (golfers, polo enthusiasts).[8][10]
- **Growth:** Population tripled in 60 years; San Roque among fastest-growing in Cádiz; 74% local hires by Sotogrande SA.[3]
- **Economy:** Tourism, real estate, services drive demand; exclusive residences attract investors/celebrities; no specific 2026 prices in data, but lavish lifestyle implied (e.g., villas €2M+ inferred from prestige).[3][6][8]
- **Density example (San Roque areas):** Varies 1,000–5,200/km².[5]

### Climate Comparison
Sotogrande enjoys a subtropical-Mediterranean climate: mild winters (avg. 12–18°C), hot summers (25–30°C), ~300 sunny days/year, low rainfall (~700mm).[1]  
**Vs. Sweden (e.g., Stockholm):** Far warmer (Sweden winter -5°C avg., summer 20°C); less rain; ideal for Swedes escaping long, dark winters—~10°C warmer year-round, enabling outdoor golf/polo 9 months vs. Sweden's 4.[1] Data stable as of 2023 estimates.
