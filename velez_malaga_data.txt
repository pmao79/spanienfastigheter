**Vélez-Málaga, in Málaga province, Andalusia, Spain, has a municipal population of approximately 85,000–87,000 residents as of recent estimates (2023–2025), covering 157–158 km² with a density of 547–556 inhabitants/km².**[1][2][4][5] The area features a growing expatriate community (11% foreigners, including Europeans), mild Mediterranean climate with 2,900 annual sunlight hours and 491mm rainfall, and key districts like Historic Center, Torre del Mar, Caleta de Velez, and Chilches.[1][2][8]

### Population Breakdown

- Gender: 49.2% male, 50.8% female (2022); 42,876 males, 44,424 females (2024).[1][2]
- Age: Average 42 years (2022); groups include 17,055 (0–17), 54,400 (18–64), 15,845 (65+).[1][2]
- Foreigners: 11% (2022), with EU (3,475), other Europe (1,684), Africa (1,887), Americas (2,568).[1][2]

- Metropolitan area: ~92,928, including nearby municipalities like Algarrobo (6,016).[3]

### Why Swedes Love It
Swedes (part of growing Northern European expat wave) are drawn to the **Costa del Sol's mild climate, affordable coastal living, and expat-friendly vibe**, with Vélez-Málaga offering value over pricier spots like Marbella. Key appeals: 300+ sunny days, English-speaking communities (UK/Irish/German alongside Scandinavians), beaches, golf, and easy flights from Stockholm (3–4 hours to Málaga Airport, 35km away). Low cost of living (~€2,000/month for couple), healthcare access, and retiree lifestyle match Swedish preferences for "second homes" in Andalusia.[2][3]

### Real Estate Market Data
